Hangul

The Korean alphabet, known as Hangul (Korean: 한글 in South Korea and 조선글 in North Korea), is the modern official writing system for the Korean language.

Modern Hangul orthography uses 24 basic letters: 14 consonant letters(ㄱ ㄴ ㄷ ㄹ ㅁ ㅂ ㅅ ㅇ ㅈ ㅊ ㅋ ㅌ ㅍ ㅎ) and 10 vowel letters(ㅏ ㅑ ㅓ ㅕ ㅗ ㅛ ㅜ ㅠ ㅡ ㅣ).
There are also 27 complex letters that are formed by combining the basic letters: 5 tense consonant letters(ㄲ ㄸ ㅃ ㅆ ㅉ), 11 complex consonant letters(ㄳ ㄵ ㄶ ㄺ ㄻ ㄼ ㄽ ㄾ ㄿ ㅀ ㅄ), and 11 complex vowel letters(ㅐ ㅒ ㅔ ㅖ ㅘ ㅙ ㅚ ㅝ ㅞ ㅟ ㅢ).

Four basic letters in the original alphabet are no longer used: 1 vowel letter(ㆍ) and 3 consonant letters(ㅿ ㆁ ㆆ).
